StockIQ: A look forward and a job announcement

Here’s a long road image I thought was a good representation of startup life. Could be the road behind, or the road ahead.

Hopefully not both. That’s potentially where you come in. But let’s provide some background.

We started StockIQ nine months ago in my brother’s apartment. Since then, we have been through three complete design overhauls, an entire backend rebuild in a new language, and more redbull than we care to keep track of.

If that’s sounding at all glamorous, I want to be clear that it is not. Startup life is not Shark Tank. It’s not ping pong and bean bags and free food and negotiating million dollar deals with venture capitalists.

Startup life is really a long slow road where the makers sit alone with their thoughts the vast majority of the time.

Occasionally we “leave the building” and listen to people tell us that our idea is crap, that we need more differentiation, or that perhaps it’s interesting but no one will pay money for this. That our deck is suspect, the space is full of incumbents, our traction is pathetic and also there are thousands of dreamers just like us out there bootstrapping startups, 90% of which completely fail.

To most of this, we have no substantive response. We have heard it the requisite hundred times and we’re still sitting here with our hoodies on working. Whatever illusions we may have had about some kind of glam startup life, those are long gone.

What we have now is singularity of focus. A certain mentality that began with coding but now applies to everything we do. When need to organize our files on google drive, we organize the hell out of them. When we do our turn washing dishes at the coworking space, we wash the hell out of them. When we prepare for a pitch, we devote our full attention to it and become what we’re doing.

Ever wonder why so many successful startups have “zen” in the name? Not us. By the time we launch we are going to be a team of monks, with no material possessions, completely one with our application.

Sounds kinda nice right? We think so.

StockIQ is always hiring for the right person. If you’d like to join the team, shoot us a dm @stock_iq. Preference will be given to programmers with two or more failed startups, 50k in credit card debt and eviction notices pending.
